Why identification matters

Each brand uses its own thread form, cover geometry, and dimensions. The correct MarinerX base unit or adapter only works when those details line up.

60-Second Identification Flow

If you want the shortest path, use this order. It narrows the field faster than browsing photos at random and keeps the measurement logic consistent with how manufacturers publish dimensions.

Step 1

Measure the nominal size

Start with the clear opening or inside thread area: usually 4, 5, 6, or 8 inches, not the outside flange.

Step 2

Check the lock type

Screw-in has full threads. Quarter-turn uses cam tabs. Pop-out has no threads.

Step 3

Match brand cues

Check thread pitch, grip shape, molded brand text, and lid color.

Step 4

Use the matrix

Find your port in the matrix below, then use that row to choose the correct Hull Dryer and adapter.

How to Measure an Inspection Port Correctly

Nominal size, outside rim diameter, and cutout size are not the same thing. For compatibility, start with the clear opening and thread pattern, then use the cutout and outside-rim numbers as confidence checks in the brand sections below.

Nominal openingClear inside diameter or advertised opening size
Outside rimOverall flange or ring dimension
Cutout holeDiameter of the hole in the deck or hull

Measurement tips

  • Measure the clear opening or inside thread area first, not the outside rim.
  • If you already know the cutout size, use it as a secondary check because brands with similar openings can still use different cutouts.
  • If the advertised size and your flange measurement do not agree, trust the clear opening and thread pattern first.

Beckson / West Marine 6-Inch Screw-In Inspection Port / Deck Plate

Usually the easiest match: a common white or beige screw-out plate with Beckson's coarse buttress thread, designed to resist cross-threading. Official Beckson dimensions for the 6-inch plate are 6-inch opening, 8-1/8-inch outside diameter, and 6-1/2-inch cutout.

Adapter needed: None. Standard Hull Dryer fits directly.

Confidence checks

  • You can see continuous coarse threads rather than quarter-turn tabs or cam slots
  • The nominal opening is 6 inches and the outside flange is about 8-1/8 inches
  • The lid uses Beckson / West Marine style grip geometry, often with dimpled or textured hand-grip areas

Unsupported Types: Quarter-Turn and Pop-Out Inspection Ports

If your port uses a cam-lock, quarter-turn latch, pry-out lid, or pop-out / snap-in retention instead of full threads, it is not currently supported. That boundary is intentional.

How to recognize unsupported types

  • No continuous screw thread around the lid or ring
  • Quarter-turn plates open with a short cam action and use slots rather than full threads
  • Low-profile pop-out plates use a pop-up lever, O-ring seal, and snap fit rather than a threaded lid
  • Pry-out or pry-off plates also have no thread engagement for an adapter to grab

Why they are not supported

  • They do not provide the same secure mechanical engagement as a supported screw-in port
  • Sealing is less predictable across quarter-turn and snap-in geometries
  • Airflow path control is harder to maintain without a fully matched threaded interface
  • Quarter-turn and low-profile pop-out systems are intentionally built to avoid cross-threading because they are not threaded systems in the first place

FAQ

How do I identify my inspection port quickly?

Start with nominal size, then determine whether it is screw-in, quarter-turn, or pop-out, then compare the thread shape and molded brand cues. If you still have two plausible matches, send photos before ordering.

What is the difference between screw-in, quarter-turn, and pop-out inspection ports?

Screw-in ports use continuous threads and several turns to lock. Quarter-turn ports use cam tabs and a short twist. Pop-out or snap-in ports rely on latch tabs or a snap fit rather than full threads.

Are different brands cross-compatible if the diameter is the same?

Usually not. Nominal diameter alone is not enough because thread pitch, lock geometry, and sealing surfaces vary by brand and port family. That is why the guide calls out both size and brand cues.

What should I do if I have a quarter-turn or pop-out port?

Do not assume compatibility. Those types are not currently supported because secure engagement and airflow control are different. Send us photos if you want us to review it, or use the waitlist placeholder above if you add one later.

How do I measure port size quickly?

Use the nominal lid opening size, typically 4 inches, 5 inches, 6 inches, or 8 inches. Avoid measuring the outer flange because flange diameter varies and can make a supported port look larger than it really is.
